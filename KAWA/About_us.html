Kothrud Animal Welfare Association (KAWA)
is a collective of people living in and around the Kothrud area who are actively involved in improving the lives of stray animals in Kothrud.

Kawa members are united in helping animals.

KAWA functions by following a well-organized process to provide prompt and effective assistance to animals in need. Here is a more detailed description of how KAWA works:


1. KAWA's network of volunteers is always on the lookout for injured or distressed animals in need of help. Anyone can call KAWA's helpline to report an animal in need.

2. Once a volunteer receives a call, they immediately post the case details in KAWA's dedicated WhatsApp group, which is closely monitored by our core team. This ensures that every case is quickly attended to and that the necessary support is provided to the volunteers.

3. KAWA's volunteers, who are located in different parts of the city, promptly attend to the location to assess the situation and provide necessary first aid to the animal. They are trained to handle different types of animals and situations, ensuring that the animal receives the best possible care.

4. Depending on the severity of the case, KAWA's team consults with experienced veterinary doctors for treatment. This ensures that the animal receives the necessary medical attention, and the best course of action is determined to ensure the animal's full recovery.

5. If the animal requires admission to a shelter, KAWA arranges for safe transportation and ensures that they receive the care they need. KAWA has partnerships with various animal shelters and rescue centers, ensuring that animals get the best care.

6. KAWA relies on donations to pay for the treatment and care of these animals. Our core team works tirelessly to raise funds and ensure that every animal receives the best care possible. KAWA is transparent about its fundraising activities and ensures that every rupee donated is used to help animals in need.



Overall, KAWA's mission is to ensure that every animal gets the love and care they deserve, and our dedicated team of volunteers and experts work tirelessly to make that happen.
